## 4. NGO Dependency Map

NGO dependency clauses — where implementer action requires prior donor approval or response:

- **BHA-015** `PROCUREMENT` `DECISION`  
  > Review of the exception(s) may slow the overall approval process and does not guarantee approval.  
  *Donor review process has downstream impact on NGO approval timeline* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-023** `PROCUREMENT` `RESTRICTION`  
  > Keep in mind that BHA cannot approve your application until the decision has been made regarding the proposed vendor.  
  *Conditional dead end: NGO application cannot proceed until donor completes vendor evaluation decision; 'cannot' used as restriction signal* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-024** `PROCUREMENT` `RESTRICTION`  
  > BHA cannot approve the use of a non-prequalified pharmaceutical vendor without sufficient information.  
  *Conditional dead end: approval blocked unless NGO provides sufficient documentation; 'sufficient information' is undefined and potentially ambiguous* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-040** `ELIGIBILITY` `RESTRICTION`  
  > Pharmaceuticals requested for USAID/BHA-supported health activities are reviewed for appropriateness for the health intervention, the situation, and the country in addition to safety, efficacy, and quality.  
  *Donor review process creates NGO dependency as approval is contingent on this review.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-041** `ELIGIBILITY` `RESTRICTION`  
  > A pharmaceutical product's inclusion on the Human EML does NOT convey blanket approval for use.  
  *Absolute prohibition on assuming blanket approval regardless of EML inclusion.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-042** `ELIGIBILITY` `RESTRICTION`  
  > USAID/BHA does not traditionally support pharmaceuticals supplied by national programs, such as the Expanded Program on Immunization (EPI) or programs focusing on family planning, HIV/AIDS, or tuberculosis.  
  *Ambiguous phrase: 'not traditionally' leaves open exceptions without defining carve-out criteria.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-048** `ELIGIBILITY` `RESTRICTION`  
  > Procurement of the pharmaceutical for the 'non-restricted use' indication is not allowed unless approved.  
  *Dead end is conditional on the NGO seeking a non-restricted use indication; donor approval required before NGO can proceed, creating NGO dependency.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-050** `ELIGIBILITY` `QUALIFIED_RESTRICTION`  
  > The products may only be used for the specified condition, unless express written USAID/BHA approval is otherwise given.  
  *Qualifier: 'unless express written USAID/BHA approval is otherwise given' softens the absolute restriction. Dead end is conditional on absence of donor written approval.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-056** `ELIGIBILITY` `RESTRICTION`  
  > BHA does not fund IARH module/kits #1A, 1B, 4, 7, and 8.  
  *Absolute exclusion of specific kit modules from funding eligibility; no exceptions stated.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-097** `PROCUREMENT` `RESTRICTION`  
  > You may not procure topical pest control until approval is received from both USAID/BHA's pharmaceutical advisor and the Agriculture team.  
  *NGO cannot procure until DONOR approval is received; creates_ngo_dependency set to true because actor is NGO but dependency is on DONOR action. Dead end is conditional on approval not yet being received.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **BHA-108** `FINANCIAL` `RESTRICTION`  
  > Obtain Agreement Officer (AO)'s approval prior to donating, transferring, or destroying pharmaceuticals.  
  *NGO cannot donate, transfer, or destroy pharmaceuticals until AO approval is granted; creates NGO dependency on donor action. Actor set to NGO as obligation falls on the implementing partner, but dependency on DONOR (AO) approval noted.* *(donor: BHA)*
- **GFFO-009** `SCOPE` `DECISION`  
  > The grant-awarding agency reserves the right to revoke the grant notification with effect for the future if it becomes apparent that the intended purpose of the grant cannot be achieved.  
  *Donor may revoke the grant if the purpose becomes unachievable; creates downstream dependency for NGO as funding can be terminated.* *(donor: GFFO)*
- **GFFO-042** `FINANCIAL` `DECISION`  
  > If grants are not used immediately upon receipt and for the intended purpose and if the grant notification has not been withdrawn or revoked, interest at a rate of five percentage points per annum above the basic rate of interest pursuant to Article 247 of the German Civil Code (BGB) may also be charged for the period from the time of payment up to the date at which the grant is put to its designated use.  
  *Donor may charge interest if funds are not used immediately; permissive right of donor but creates financial risk/dependency for NGO.* *(donor: GFFO)*

**Key finding:** **13 clauses** create NGO dependency — where implementer progress is blocked pending a donor action (BHA: 11, GFFO: 2). These are operational bottlenecks and should be tracked in project workplans.
