# Regulatory Mapping Analysis Report
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Donors analyzed:** USAID/BHA, ECHO
**Documents:**
- BHA: *USAID BHA PMC Guidance Dec 2023* (114 clauses)
- ECHO: *Provisions on Medical and Food Supplies EN 2025 Technical Update Final* (27 clauses)

> **Note on scale:** ECHO's document is 4x shorter than BHA's. All comparisons use percentages unless otherwise noted.

---

## 1. Overall Tier Distribution

| Tier | BHA (n=114) | ECHO (n=27) |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| RESTRICTION | 89% (101) | 70% (19) |
| HIGH_RISK | 7% (8) | 4% (1) |
| DECISION | 4% (4) | **19%** (5) |
| QUALIFIED_RESTRICTION | 1% (1) | 7% (2) |

**Key finding:** BHA is overwhelmingly mandatory — 89% of its clauses are hard restrictions with no discretion. ECHO has nearly 5x more DECISION-tier clauses proportionally (19% vs 4%) and 7x more QUALIFIED_RESTRICTIONs (7% vs 1%). ECHO leaves substantially more room for implementer judgment.

## 5. CONDITIONAL Dead Ends

Obligations that are binding only within specific conditions — important for compliance scoping.

### BHA (17)

All 17 fall in ELIGIBILITY (13) or PROCUREMENT (3), with one spanning both:

**PROCUREMENT (3):**
- BHA cannot approve application until vendor decision is made
- BHA cannot approve use of a non-prequalified pharmaceutical vendor without sufficient information
- Topical pest control may not be procured without dual approval (pharmaceutical advisor + Agriculture team)

**ELIGIBILITY (14) — pharmaceutical-specific use restrictions:**
- Artesunate: injectable only for severe malaria
- Chloroquine: only for P. vivax where not resistant
- Primaquine: only for radical cure of P. vivax and P. ovale, 14 days
- Quinine: only for severe malaria, in combination with doxycycline
- SPAQ-CO: only for Seasonal Malaria Chemoprevention in eligible areas per WHO
- Pentamidine: only for T. b. gambiense
- Suramin sodium: only for initial phase of T. b. rhodesiense
- Trisodium citrate dihydrate: may be replaced by sodium bicarbonate only if immediately consumed
- Hydralazine: restricted to gestational hypertension
- Methyldopa: restricted to gestational hypertension
- Misoprostol vaginal tablet: restricted to induction of labor where facilities available
- Nifedipine Immediate Release: restricted to preterm labor without delivery
- Non-restricted use procurement: not allowed unless approved
- Product use: only for specified condition unless express written BHA approval

### ECHO (1)

- When not purchasing through an HPC, must procure through pre-certified suppliers *(the conditional mirror of the UNCONDITIONAL procurement channel requirement)*

## 6. AMBIGUOUS Dead Ends — Flagged for Human Audit

These clauses could not be definitively classified and must not enter any compliance baseline without review.

**BHA:**
> *[ELIGIBILITY]* "USAID/BHA does not traditionally support pharmaceuticals supplied by national programs, such as the Expanded Program on Immunization (EPI) or programs focusing on family planning, HIV/AIDS, or tuberculosis."

The word "traditionally" makes this ambiguous — it implies past practice rather than a hard rule, but the practical effect may be binding.

**ECHO:**
> *[ELIGIBILITY]* "While no derogation from the minimum quality assurances for medical supplies may be granted, where the humanitarian organisation, for circumstances beyond its control, is unable to demonstrate compliance with internationally accepted product standards, it may demonstrate instead that the supplies offer the best quality available."

This clause straddles UNCONDITIONAL (no derogation from minimums) and DECISION (force majeure carve-out). Its actual constraint level depends on interpretation of "circumstances beyond its control."

---

## 7. QUALIFIED_RESTRICTION Clauses

Low volume in both donors — only 3 total — but these represent softened mandatory language worth monitoring.

**BHA (1):**
> *[ELIGIBILITY]* "The products may only be used for the specified condition, unless express written USAID/BHA approval is otherwise given."

**ECHO (2):**
> *[PROCUREMENT]* "Must ensure adequate provisions for destruction of recalled or expired supplies... and when possible internationally recognised best practices."

> *[PROCUREMENT]* "Whenever possible and advisable... priority must be given to purchases in the country of operation or in neighbouring countries."

ECHO's QUALIFIED_RESTRICTIONs use "when possible" / "whenever possible and advisable" — conditional on context. BHA's is a hard rule with an explicit approval escape valve.

## 8. DECISION Nodes — Implementer Discretion

### BHA (4) — narrow, exception-driven

| Domain | Clause |
|--------|--------|
| PROCUREMENT | Review of the exception(s) may slow the overall approval process and does not guarantee approval. |
| PROCUREMENT | If an exception is approved, you may proceed with procurement. |
| PROCUREMENT | Additionally, you may select a different prequalified pharmaceutical vendor. |
| REPORTING | Additional guidance may be found in the Indicator Handbook. |

BHA's decision nodes are not genuine discretion — they are escape valves contingent on BHA approval. The implementer has no independent authority; they are waiting for permission.

### ECHO (5) — condition-triggered autonomy

| Domain | Clause |
|--------|--------|
| PROCUREMENT | May use negotiated procedure with single offer when using an HPC. |
| PROCUREMENT | In that case, pre-qualification of supplies not required. |
| ELIGIBILITY | If unable to demonstrate compliance with international standards due to circumstances beyond control, may demonstrate "best quality available" instead. |
| PROCUREMENT | For urgent contracts or contracts under EUR 300,000, org may self-certify quantity and quality. |
| PROCUREMENT | May apply negotiated single-offer procedure for food supplies from an HPC. |

ECHO's decision nodes are genuinely operational — specific thresholds (EUR 300k, urgency, HPC context) trigger real implementer authority without requiring donor approval.

**Summary contrast:** BHA decisions = permission-seeking. ECHO decisions = condition-triggered autonomy.

## 9. HIGH_RISK Clauses

Softened advisory language ("should", "you are advised", "expected to") that carries implicit compliance risk.

### BHA (8) — all but one in PROCUREMENT

- Provide evidence of extensive vendor evaluation
- Physically inspect vendor facility (storage, cold chain, pest control, etc.)
- Refer to updated Prequalified Pharmaceutical Vendors list before requesting procurements
- Where appropriate, use current internationally standardized pharmaceutical kits
- Prolonged crisis projects should evolve from large standardized kits to targeted approaches
- Therapeutic foods are not pharmaceuticals — should not appear on PMC template
- For animal health services, use market-based payment modalities connecting AHSPs to local PVPs
- *[FINANCIAL]* Expected to manage purchases of medical equipment and supplies appropriately and account for unused commodities at project end

### ECHO (1)

- *[PROCUREMENT]* For medical devices, quality criteria should be aligned to risk classification.

BHA's HIGH_RISK cluster is concentrated in vendor management and commodity classification — areas where mis-steps are likely to trigger compliance review. ECHO's single HIGH_RISK is a general quality alignment statement.

## 10. Cross-Donor Overlap Assessment

Direct cross-donor pooling (grouping equivalent obligations by domain) is currently feasible only in **PROCUREMENT**, where both donors have meaningful coverage.

| Domain | BHA coverage | ECHO coverage | Pooling feasibility |
|--------|-------------|---------------|---------------------|
| PROCUREMENT | 36% (41 clauses) | 67% (18 clauses) | **Viable** |
| ELIGIBILITY | 26% (30 clauses) | 15% (4 clauses) | Limited — ECHO too thin |
| REPORTING | 21% (24 clauses) | 7% (2 clauses) | Not meaningful |
| FINANCIAL | 7% (8 clauses) | — | BHA only |
| RECORD_KEEPING | 5% (6 clauses) | 11% (3 clauses) | Too thin on both sides |
| SAFEGUARDING | 4% (5 clauses) | — | BHA only |

Within PROCUREMENT, the shared UNCONDITIONAL dead ends are the strongest pooling candidates:
- BHA: ARV restrictions, disinfectant classification rules, commodity template rules (5 of 10 UNCONDITIONAL)
- ECHO: HPC or pre-certified supplier requirement (1 of 1 UNCONDITIONAL)

These are structurally different obligations — BHA's are commodity-specific, ECHO's is channel-specific. They may not normalize into shared canonical forms but represent complementary compliance baselines.

## 11. Notable Data Quality Flags

1. **BHA duplicate:** Two UNCONDITIONAL PROCUREMENT dead ends contain identical text about ARV/PEP kit authorization. Flag for deduplication before pooling.
2. **ECHO thin coverage:** 27 clauses is a small sample. Absence of FINANCIAL and SAFEGUARDING domains in ECHO likely reflects document scope, not policy absence — these obligations may exist in other ECHO documents not yet processed.
3. **SCOPE domain:** No clauses assigned to SCOPE in either document. Either the taxonomy's SCOPE signals aren't present in these documents, or the classifier is underassigning this domain. Worth a targeted review.
4. **BHA QUALIFIED_RESTRICTION undercount (1 clause):** Given the volume of pharmaceutical-specific language with conditional exceptions, the classifier may be over-assigning CONDITIONAL dead ends vs. QUALIFIED_RESTRICTIONs. The distinction is subtle and worth auditing on the ELIGIBILITY CONDITIONAL set.
